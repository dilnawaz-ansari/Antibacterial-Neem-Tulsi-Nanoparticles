# Antibacterial Activity of Neem and Tulsi Using Silver Nanoparticles

## Published Research | IJPMN Journal | August 2024

**Author:** Dilnawaz Ansari  
**Institution:** Institute of Biosciences and Technology, MGM University, Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar, India  
**Journal:** International Journal of Public Mental Health and Neurosciences  
**ISSN:** 2394-4668 | Volume 11, Issue 2, August 2024  

---

## Abstract

This study investigates the antibacterial potential of 
*Azadirachta indica* (Neem) and *Ocimum sanctum* (Tulsi) 
through green synthesis of silver nanoparticles (AgNPs). 
Colour change from pale yellow to brown confirmed 
successful AgNP synthesis. Both plants demonstrated 
antibacterial activity against *E. coli*.

---

## Key Findings

| Plant | Antibacterial Activity | Zone of Clearance |
|-------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Neem (*Azadirachta indica*) | Yes | 2 cm |
| Tulsi (*Ocimum sanctum*) | Yes | 1.5 cm |

---

## Methodology Overview

### Neem Silver Nanoparticle Synthesis
- 15g neem leaves in 50ml distilled water
- Water bath at 60°C for 25 minutes
- Mixed with 1mM AgNO3 solution
- Colour change: pale yellow → brown = AgNP confirmed
- Centrifuged at 2000 rpm for 15 minutes

### Tulsi Silver Nanoparticle Synthesis
- 10g tulsi leaves crushed with 100ml distilled water
- Boiled at 80°C for 10 minutes
- Mixed with 1mM AgNO3 solution
- Centrifuged at 10,000 rpm for 20 minutes

### Antibacterial Testing
- Pure E. coli culture streaked on nutrient agar
- Disk diffusion method
- Incubated for 24 hours
- Zone of clearance measured

---

## Nutrient Agar Composition

| Component | Quantity |
|-----------|----------|
| Peptone | 0.5 gm |
| NaCl | 0.5 gm |
| Yeast Extract | 0.3 gm |
| Agar-Agar | 1.8 gm |
| Distilled Water | 100 ml |

---

## Results

- Both Neem and Tulsi showed **confirmed antibacterial 
activity** against *E. coli*
- Neem showed larger zone of clearance (2 cm) vs 
Tulsi (1.5 cm)
- Green synthesis of AgNPs successfully achieved 
from both medicinal plants
